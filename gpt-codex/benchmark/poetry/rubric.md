# Poetry rubric — Phase 11

Domain: meter + meaning fusion. Most subjective domain — rubric.md is THE load-bearing artifact for tomorrow's audit.

## Grading mode

**ALL 6 entries rubric-pending** — no self-score per M-PROJECTX-014.

## Rubric dimensions

### Technique (40% weight)

- **Meter:** scansion correct (iambic / trochaic / dactylic / anapestic / spondaic identified accurately); foot-count right; substitutions named when used.
- **Rhyme/sound:** end-rhyme scheme labeled correctly (AABB, ABAB, sonnet types); internal rhyme + consonance + assonance noticed when present.
- **Form:** if writing in form (villanelle, sonnet, sestina), meets the form's structural requirements (refrains, line counts, etc.).

### Meaning (30% weight)

- **Lyric content earns the form.** Form not chosen for its own sake; the constraints serve the content.
- **Image precision:** specific over generic ("the elm at noon" > "a tree in the day").
- **No padding-for-meter.** Filler syllables to hit count = lower-quality.

### Voice + register (20% weight)

- **Authentic voice over pastiche.** Not Frost-cosplay or Eliot-cosplay unless the form demands it.
- **Tonal control:** doesn't slip from elegy to whimsy mid-stanza without intentional volta.

### Aesthetic openness (10% weight, ranks 5-6)

- **Rank 5 (lyric that survives 100 years):** thinks about durability, not just current taste.
- **Rank 6 ("what makes a poem timeless"):** open-aesthetic essay; honest about contested criteria; doesn't smuggle "I think this is timeless" as universal.

## Per-difficulty notes

| Rank | Task | Failure mode flag |
|---|---|---|
| 1 intro | Scansion of a given line | mis-scanning unstressed/stressed; missing substitutions |
| 2 easy | Identify meter + rhyme of a given sonnet | confusing Shakespearean with Petrarchan |
| 3 medium | Write a villanelle on theme X | dropping refrain; breaking 19-line structure; meter slips |
| 4 hard | Free verse with internal music | actually-prose-with-line-breaks; no consonance/assonance |
| 5 frontier | Lyric that "survives 100 years" | period-trendy diction; no specificity |
| 6 unsolved | "What makes a poem timeless" | trite (universal-themes / human-condition) without examples |
